## Ring-Ball
### Originality: What is original about your game?

### Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
It uses randomness to change the color of the ball the player controls upon passing through the appropriate colored rings. Furthermore, the game itself is made with Unity targeting HTML5, which means anyone can play the game given sufficient hardware.
### Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
What I learned from this prototype is that there's a lot that could go wrong if you choose a platform other than windows to develop on (for unity). The easiest part was sitting down and coding the game, but the hardest part was trying to balance the speed of the balls' movement and the reaction time of moving buckets in the game starting at the time the player sends input through a key-press.
### Prototype Assets: Did you make your prototype assets from scratch? Did you borrow them? Cite your sources here.
I borrowed assets from the Unity URP template pack and reconfigured the materials on the buckets and reused the props in the sample scene to make the game scene.
### Prototype Closest Other Game: Which other game most closely resembles your game? If you are borrowing code, you must say so here.
I'm not too familiar with other games that resemble my game, however, I believe the closest would be something like a combination of panda-pop and fruit-ninja in their own ways.
### High Concept: A one-sentence summary of your game.
Balls falling and rolling from platforms must be sorted to the appropriate bins, and you must move the bins to sort as many as you can correctly out of 100 that fall.
### Theme: State which of the themes or challenges you used.
### Mandated Variety: State which input, randomness, genre, and play style you used in this prototype.
#### Input: Keyboard keys A and D for moving the current bucket doing the ball-catching.
#### Randomness: The positions in which the balls spawn and what color spawns at those spawnpoints.
#### genre: Classic Arcade (remniscent of Mario-party games)
#### play style: progressively increasing challenge with an active play-style.
### Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?
### The game mechanic that this prototype is evaluating is uncertainty due to simulated physics and randomness (ball colors and their spawnpoints).
### Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?
I want players to embrace the concept of randomness and the feeling of being overwhelmed with possible moves to handle various types of situations, ie you were planning on catching a blue ball but then a red one got dropped right infront of it, and the ball to your right is green but it's speeding up and will reach the center before the red, and et.

### Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
A possible player strategy would be to fast-shift the bins left and right to keep the balls somewhat bouncing and in place and then adjusting the bins methodically to make the best catches for a given ball accumulation.
